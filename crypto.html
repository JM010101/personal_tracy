Yes, I read the Satoshi Nakamoto Bitcoin paper in October of 2008. I’m still not interested in that stuff. Also, “Not your keys, not your ‘crypto’.” And if you do mess with cryptocurrencies, I hope you are really good at IT stuff like backups, keeping track of passwords, not letting in malware or ransomware, etc.

I’m no mathematician and I definitely avoid implementing my own crypto but I have done a lot of work with implementing cryptography as a security engineer and architect.

I have been a crypto user since 1993 when I discovered PGP. Nowadays, I use GPG. You can find my keys on keyservers going back many years.

You can find my GPG public key fingerprint in google via email list archives from many years ago.

I read Schneier’s “Applied Cryptography” back in 1996.

I am a CACert assurer.

I have taken university classes on encryption and done the math manually on (very small) integers to implement RSA as well as prove that one time pads are mathematically secure (when properly implemented, of course).

I am on keybase.

I am on signal.

I used dm-crypt and then LUKS to encrypt my servers and mobile devices.
